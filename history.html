Geography

The town of Fort Lee is located at the top of Hudson Palisades on the peninsula between Hackensack and the Hudson River. The area is sepearated by the bisection traffic in and out of the George Washington Bridge to New York City. It's neighboring towns are Cliffside Park, Edgewater, Englewood, Englewood Cliffs, Leonia, Ridgefield, and the Washington Heights neighborhood of Upper Manhattan.

Revolutionary War

In 1776, the first poeple to have lived in Fort Lee were the Lenni Lenape Indians. After Captain Henry Hudson recorded reference of it in 1609, Stephen Bourdette purchased 400 acres of it along the known town of present-day Edgewater in 1756. Bourdette's home was the only stone house within the mile radius and later became to serve as General George Washington's headquaters during the American Revolution.

  • Originally named Fort Constitution but later named after Major General Charles Lee (February 6, 1732 - October 2, 1782); Fort Lee is the sister garrison of Fort Washington.
  • The Battle of Fort Lee (appr. Nov 19, 1776 - Nov 20, 1776) was an American loss to British general, Charles Lord Cornwallis who led 5,000 soilders to invade the fort that had already been evacuated by orders of American Patriot Maj. Gen. Nathaniel Greene. 2,000 American soldiers retreated to Hackensack where George Washington awaited them. When the British arrived, they were found with 50 cannons, 1,000 barrels of flower, barrels of ammunition, and plenty of other supplies left by the fleeting Revolutuionaries. Twelve drunken Americans soldiers were captured along with 150 other prisoners.
  • No deaths or injuries were reported.
Source: Battlefields, FortLeeNJ, & Revolutionary War

Film Industry

Though shadowed by the western popularities such as Hollywood and Los Angeles, the origins of cinema come from the area of Fort Lee, New Jersey. The famous inventor, Thomas Edison, even owned a studio company named the "Black Maria" in nearby West Orange considered to be 'America's first movie studio'. Knowns for its beautiful and natural scenery, Fort Lee served as a key site for film productions such as 'Within Our Gates' by Oscar Micheaux. As the rise of Fort Lee's popularity in the film industry peaked, Nestor Studios of Bayonne, built the first studio in Hollywood, CA in 1911 and later merged with Universal Studios.

  • Motion pictures were invented and first produced at Thomas Edison’s laboratory and studio in West Orange.
  • Coined the term "cliffhanger" due to the cliff scenery of the Palisades.
  • Fort Lee was once home to a number of film studios, including Fox Film Corporation and Universal.
Source: NJ.gov & BBC

Mini-NY

Given its close location and rich history, Fort Lee is considered to be urban/suburban mimic of New York City. With its range of diverse cultures, busy streets into the late night, and modern architecture, this city is but a carbon copy of its neighbor across the Hudson River. For more information about housings, demographics and more visit the New York Times article(2023) where several quotes are displayed from down below.
