Carpenters Grove, Palisades Interstate Park Commission

Carpenter’s Grove is a small picnic area south of Ross Dock Picnic Area in Fort Lee available for group use by groups up to 75 by reservation from May 1 – October 31. The site is furnished with 15 stationary picnic tables, grills, port-a-johns, and garbage and recycling cans.

The Palisades Interstate Park Commission, formed in 1900, operates the Palisades Interstate Park in New Jersey and the State Parks and Historic Sites in New York’s Palisades Region with several different hike routes with beautiful sightlines.

More information on Palisades Interstate Park can be found at njpalisades.org.

Fort Lee Historic Park, Palisades Interstate Park Commission

Fort Lee Historic Park is set on 33 landscaped acres atop the Palisades just south of the George Washington Bridge, with spectacular overlooks of the Hudson, Manhattan, and the George Washington Bridge. There are a Visitor Center and Museum, reconstructed 18th Century soldier hut and campsite, and reconstructed gun batteries. Fort Lee has been named as a significant stop on the Hudson River Valley National Heritage Area Revolutionary War trail.

The Visitor Center is open Wednesday–Sunday, 10:00 AM – 4:45 PM.
